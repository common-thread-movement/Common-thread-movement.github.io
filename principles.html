The Civic Code

The following principles form the moral and civic foundation of the Common Thread movement. They are not laws—they are commitments that strengthen the nation from the inside out.

1. Merit Over Tribe

Identity earns no virtue; actions do. A nation thrives when people rise by character, not category.

2. Duty Before Dominance

Power pursued for its own sake devours the nation that grants it. Duty protects what dominance destroys.

3. Integrity as Social Glue

When honesty collapses, institutions hollow out from the inside. Shared truth is the basis of shared trust.

4. A Nation Is a Moral Field

Everything citizens do adds or subtracts from the nation’s moral space. We are responsible for what we shape.

5. Shared Responsibility, Shared Reward

A nation is a cooperative project, not a competition of factions.

6. Unity Through Voluntary Commitment

Real unity is chosen through conscience, not coerced through force.

7. Majority/Minority Fluidity

No group holds the majority forever. Humility is national survival.

8. Citizenship Is an Active Verb

A democracy survives only when citizens participate with seriousness, courage, and duty.

9. Truth Over Partisanship

Parties are temporary. The nation is permanent.

10. Human Dignity Is Non-Negotiable

Rights do not come from government; government exists to guard them.

11. Moral Courage Over Social Comfort

Going beyond tribe requires bravery. Conscience is higher than conformity.

12. The Common Thread

A nation succeeds only when its people see themselves as part of one shared story.
