Essays

Below are the major essays of the Common Thread Civic Movement. Tap a title to read the full version.

Bless G-d, Bless America, Bless Wherever You're From

A civic-nationalist essay exploring unity, conscience, responsibility, and why a nation’s real strength comes from voluntary duty rather than dominance.

Civic Duty & the Fluid Majority-Minority Principle

An explanation of why healthy civic identity requires the willingness to move between majority and minority stances — not out of weakness, but moral strength.

The Collapse of Civic Identity

A critique of how tribalism and partisan identity distort character, suppress conscience, and undermine the moral rhythm required for a stable nation.

A Moral Constitution From the Founding Documents

A construction of a civic moral framework using direct quotations from American founding documents — outlining a working “moral constitution.”
