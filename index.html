➜ Click here to begin

The Common Thread is a civic identity built around unity, conscience, responsibility, integrity, and grounded awareness. It reflects a belief that the strength of a nation depends not only on its laws and institutions, but on the character, steadiness, and responsibility of its citizens.

This identity is voluntary, non-partisan, and non-sectarian. It is not a movement to join, but a posture to live — a way of standing in the world with dignity, honesty, and civic maturity.
